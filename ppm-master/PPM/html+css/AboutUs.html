Who are we?

We consider that all those affected by eating disorders and issues, including family, friends, parents, and support professionals, should have access to resources.

Eating disorders are usually about food; rather, sufferers generally utilise food as a coping mechanism for underlying emotional issues or, in more serious forms, mental illness.

The 5 P's:

Passion: You have motivated us to make a difference because of your passion.

People: We are sympathetic, understanding, and nonjudgmental individuals.

Potential: We recognise all of your abilities and accomplishments and encourage you to continue to grow.

Partnership:We are welcoming and open, and we value your input and experience.

Professional: We are experts in eating disorders and mental health who are ethical and accountable.

Our Timeline:

2004: Cathy Cleary MBE, our Founder and past sufferer of an eating disorder as a youngster created First Steps.

2008: First Steps received the BBC Children in Need's first "significant" funding.

2009: The Volunteering England Fund has provided a three-year grant. The expansion of First Steps necessitated a larger area. We relocated to Osmaston Road in Derby, which allowed us to provide individualised help to up to 20 young people each year. The number of volunteers has climbed from 5 to 24.

2012: Initiated offering CPD-accredited training to professionals in the NHS, schools, and health and private practice counsellors.

2013: PQASSO, the National Community Voluntary Organizations Trusted Charity Quality Assurance Framework, was awarded to First Steps.

2015: First Steps was awarded a two-and-a-half-year NHS contract to provide support to the University of Nottingham and Nottingham Trent University students.

2016: The Queen's Award for Voluntary Services was given to First Steps. This is the highest award given to volunteer groups in the United Kingdom, and it is given to recognise the great work that they do in their areas. It's known as the MBE for volunteer organisations.

2017: The PQASSO Gold Standard was attained. Through The Kings Fund, I won the GSK IMPACT Award. In the area of "Supported Self Care". We received a distinguished Health Service Journal (HSJ) Value Award

2019: Following our members' approval at the 2018/19 Annual General Meeting, we became First Steps ED and a Charitable Incorporated Organization. Our volunteer contributions were once again recognised when we were named Volunteer Team of the Year at the East Midlands Charity Awards.

Present:

We keep growing as a non-profit organisation led by people who have firsthand experience with eating disorders and co-morbid mental health issues or have cared for someone who has.

We're finding new ways to adapt to a changing environment for mental health and eating disorders, as well as working closely with our partners in healthcare and education to advance research, policy, and service co-production so that more individuals and families receive the support they need and deserve from integrated services focused on joined-up care.

Our Team:

Our award-winning and quality-assured support to those living with eating disorders, as well as parents and caregivers and a network of professionals, is made possible by the unwavering hard work, devotion, and enthusiasm of our trustees, volunteers, partners, funders, and wonderful staff.

We owe our ability to grow and continue to be the Midlands' leading eating disorder charity to our employees and stakeholders.

Our crew is well-versed in eating problems. Our team brings a wealth of knowledge and experience to the table, allowing us to run smoothly while relying on the help and support of our fantastic volunteers.
