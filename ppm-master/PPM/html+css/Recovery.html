Recovery

What we can do to help you recover

Visit first steps...

To different people, recovery means something different.It's possible that you won't have any more thoughts and behaviors connected to your eating disorder.You may still have ideas and behaviours, but they will be less frequent. They may also have a less significant impact on people ’s lives. Your perception of your relationship with food and your beliefs on rehabilitation may develop over time.

Believe

Believe you don't have a problem, that your actions are beneficial to you, think your eating disorder is soothing, safe, or even exhilarating; that you are afraid of the changes that will accompany recovery. Even when you feel ready to attempt, whatever recovery looks like to you, it can take a long time to get there. It's possible that you'll have to think in years rather than weeks or months.

Don't be worried

If you're terrified of losing or gaining weight, worried about losing control, or if your eating disorder is a large part of everyone’s life and personality, you're not sure who you are without it, recovery can be frightening. If you've attempted to heal before and failed, you may begin to believe that you're entirely helpless. But, even if it takes a long time, it is possible to feel better.

Media should stop doing this

Media images are frequently manipulated and refined, emphasising youth and skinniness. We've all come of age seeing all these pictures so frequently that we don't even think about them anymore. In the media, women are frequently sexualized and utilised as sex objects to promote items. Gender stereotypes and demands are also influenced by the media. The media frequently portrays larger bodies in a bad light, which might influence our perceptions of others. Whenever we see women on Television or in movies, they are typically younger and thinner than their male co-stars.

Food insecurity

We recently discussed "food insecurity".  This denotes scarcity of or restriction on food. Growing up in a family with limited food or in a large family where there is always a race to grab the most food can have an impact on how we view food as children. Not having enough food, for instance, may lead you to hide or stockpile food, eat in secret, or excessively eat.
